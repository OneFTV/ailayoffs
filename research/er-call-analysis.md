## Key Findings

1. **The "AI Swap" pattern is real:** Intuit's 1:1 swap (fire 1,800, hire 1,800 AI roles) is the most explicit example, but Cisco, Salesforce, and Workday followed similar patterns.

2. **CEO language is the tell:** When CEOs say "efficiency," "restructuring for growth areas," or "investing in AI capabilities" in the same announcement as layoffs, the correlation is strong even without explicit admission.

3. **Two waves:**
   - **Wave 1 (2023):** Framed as "pandemic over-hiring correction" â€” but companies simultaneously launched AI products. (Salesforce, Zoom, Twilio, Dell)
   - **Wave 2 (2024):** Much more explicit AI connection. (Intuit, Cisco, eBay, PayPal, Workday, Bumble)

4. **Stack Overflow is the canary:** Perhaps the clearest example of a company whose core business was directly disrupted by AI (ChatGPT), leading to layoffs. Traffic down 35-50%, then 28% workforce cut.

5. **Block/Square trajectory is alarming:** Jack Dorsey's 50% workforce reduction by 2026, explicitly citing AI, may preview what many companies will do.
